# Instructor Feedback on Initial Dataset Submission

Overall, I can tell you have a genuine interest in this topic and there are moments in the documentation where you're thinking critically about what this data can and can't tell us. Your questions about what exactly counts as a "playlist appearance," whether it's weighted by playlist size, and whether it's global or regional are exactly the right ones to be asking. Your observation about a potential positive feedback loop pushing already popular songs back to users is also interesting and worth developing. The instinct to approach this as an audit rather than building from scratch is also appropriate given the dataset you chose. But there are some significant gaps in the submission that need to be addressed before this project can move forward meaningfully.

**1. The Audit Hasn't Actually Happened**

The most significant issue is that while you've framed your approach as "auditing," what you've actually done is clean and filter the dataset rather than verify it. Auditing means something specific in this course: tracing the data back to its source, verifying its accuracy, and trying to understand how it was created. In my earlier feedback on your topic selection I gave you specific guidance on how to approach this, so please go back and refer to that document. For this dataset in particular, that would mean questions like: Who created this Kaggle dataset and how? Was it scraped directly from Spotify? If so, when, and what was the methodology? Can you spot-check individual entries against what Spotify currently shows? What might have changed since the data was collected? Is there data missing? Without doing this work, any analysis built on top of the dataset is on uncertain ground.

The cleaning decisions you did make also need more explanation. Filtering out non-English songs is a significant interpretive step. Why does the language of a song matter for what you're trying to study? What does "English" even mean in the context of global music streaming, where a song might have lyrics in multiple languages or be sung in English by a non-English artist? Similarly, deciding which features are "redundant" or not worth keeping requires a clear rationale. What exactly makes a feature redundant for your purposes? These aren't minor technical choices, they are interpretive decisions that shape what the dataset can say, and they need to be explained and justified.

**2. The Cultural Question Needs More Focus**

The other significant gap is that it's not yet clear what cultural phenomenon you're actually trying to study. You write that platforms like Spotify reduce music to metrics, which is an interesting critical observation, but it's the beginning of a question rather than the question itself. Are you interested in how users create playlists and what that reveals about music curation? Are you interested in how popularity gets defined and reinforced through streaming data? Are you interested in whether streaming makes music tastes more homogenous over time? These are all different projects with different implications for what data you need and how you'd analyze it.

Your observation about the positive feedback loop is the most analytically interesting thing in the submission, but as you note it's very difficult to assess from this data alone. One direction that might actually be more tractable would be looking at variation between playlists rather than what they have in common. There's a hypothesis in the literature that streaming platforms make music taste more homogenous. Does data like this actually support that, or does the variation between playlists tell a more complicated story?

**3. Next Steps**

The plan to use the Spotify API to expand the dataset is a reasonable direction, though I'd flag a few things. The API has significant rate limits and requires authentication, so the process is more involved than the documentation suggests. Rather than using raw `requests`, I'd recommend looking at `Spotipy` [https://spotipy.readthedocs.io/en/2.26.0/](https://spotipy.readthedocs.io/en/2.26.0/), which is a Python library built specifically for the Spotify API and will make the process considerably more manageable. I'm not entirely convinced that the language detection is that interpretative but if you want to continue with that approach there are some open source libraries like `langdetect` [https://github.com/Mimino666/langdetect](https://github.com/Mimino666/langdetect). But again there's the broader question of why does language matter or what do you think it represents.

Overall, I think this project has real potential if you can resolve these two core issues: doing the actual verification and provenance work on the existing dataset, and clarifying what cultural phenomenon you're most interested in exploring. Once you have answers to both of those questions, the path forward becomes much clearer. Please do revisit my earlier feedback on your topic selection, and don't hesitate to reach out if you want to talk through the focus question before you move into the scaling phase.
