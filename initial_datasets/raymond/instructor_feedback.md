# Instructor Feedback on Initial Dataset Submission

Overall, there are some genuinely interesting moments in this submission. Your observation about Pumped Up Kicks is a good concrete example of the tension between what audio features capture and what a song actually means culturally, and your reflection on the limits of scaling human verification is honest and worth developing. The focus on lesser-known songs is a distinctive angle that sets your project apart from others in your group. That said, there are several significant issues I need to flag.

**1. The Dataset Hasn't Been Audited**

As with the audit and augment approach more broadly, what you've done here is clean and filter the dataset rather than verify it. You note that you chose this Kaggle dataset partly because of its popularity but that's not the same as auditing it yourself. I gave specific guidance in my earlier feedback on your topic selection on how to approach this, so please go back and refer to that document. The questions you need to be asking include: Who created this dataset and how? Was it actually scraped from the Spotify API as the author claims, and can you verify that? Are there songs you'd expect to find that are missing? Can you spot-check specific entries against what Spotify currently shows? Your own documentation acknowledges that many of the features are "estimated" by algorithms, which is exactly the kind of thing the audit is supposed to help you interrogate. That's a good instinct, it just needs to be followed through.

I'm also curious how confident you are in the "people listening" figure you're using to define lesser-known songs, and how exactly you're deriving that from the popularity score in the dataset. Spotify's popularity score is not a direct count of listeners, it's a proprietary metric that weights recency and other factors. Understanding what it actually measures, and whether it maps onto what you mean by "lesser-known," is a core part of the audit you need to do.

**2. The Focus Needs More Clarity and Independence**

I want to raise something directly: portions of your submission read very similarly to your groupmates' submissions, particularly around the English language filtering rationale and the discussion of valence and emotional interpretation. I understand that you've been working from a shared group document, but your individual submission needs to reflect your own distinct project and thinking. More importantly, the framing around valence and emotional interpretation doesn't obviously connect to your stated interest in lesser-known songs. Why does emotional interpretation matter specifically for the songs you're focusing on? What is it about less popular music that interests you, and how would data help you explore that?

The English-only filtering also needs a clearer rationale given your focus. If anything, lesser-known songs might be disproportionately non-English, since international music is often underrepresented in mainstream streaming metrics. Filtering them out could actually work against your stated interest and that tension is worth thinking through carefully.

**3. Next Steps**

The next steps section doesn't connect clearly to your focus. The plan to merge with a larger dataset or use the Spotify API is reasonable in principle, but the more pressing question is what you're actually trying to find out about lesser-known songs and how any of these methods would help you find it. Before scaling, I'd strongly encourage you to spend time clarifying that core question. What would be interesting or surprising to discover about this music? Is it that lesser-known songs have different audio feature distributions than popular ones? That certain genres are systematically underrepresented in popularity metrics? That the relationship between audio features and popularity breaks down at the margins? Once you have a clearer answer to that question, the methodological decisions (what data to collect, how to filter, what to compare) will follow much more naturally.

Overall, there's a genuinely interesting project here if you can develop it more independently and ground it in a clearer research focus. The starting point is doing the verification work that auditing requires and articulating more precisely what draws you to this particular corner of the Spotify ecosystem.
