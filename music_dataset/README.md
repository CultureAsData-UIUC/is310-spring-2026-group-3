# Music Dataset Project

## Dataset

I created a dataset of 75 songs. Each row represents one song, and each column shows different information about the song.

The dataset includes:

* Song
* Artist
* Genre
* Popularity
* Mood
* Theme
* Energy

This structure combines basic information with human interpretation.

---

## What I Did

I chose music as my topic because music reflects emotions, identity, and culture.

I followed a hybrid approach:

* Used a structure similar to existing datasets (like Spotify/Kaggle)
* Added my own columns such as mood, theme, and energy

This helped me go beyond basic data and include meaning.

---

## Tools Used

* Excel / Google Sheets
* Music platforms like Spotify

These tools helped organize the data, but I manually decided mood and theme.

---

## Decisions

* Selected popular songs from different genres
* Limited dataset to 75 songs for better accuracy
* Used simple categories for mood, theme, and energy

---

## Challenges

* Mood and theme are subjective
* Some songs fit multiple categories

To solve this, I chose the main mood/theme and stayed consistent across all songs.

---

## Patterns I Noticed

* Pop songs mostly focus on love and relationships
* Hip-hop often focuses on success and lifestyle
* High-energy songs are usually fast and upbeat

---

## Next Steps

In the next stage, I plan to grow this dataset and also improve its quality.

First, I will collect more songs (500–1,000) using tools like the Spotify API. This will help me move from a small manual dataset to a larger computational dataset.

Second, I want to improve how mood and theme are assigned. Instead of only manual work, I will use AI tools to analyze lyrics and patterns in music to predict mood automatically.

I will also compare my manual labels with AI-generated labels to see differences. This will help me understand how human interpretation and machine decisions can vary.

Another step is to add more features like release year, tempo, and popularity trends over time. This will make the dataset more useful for analysis.

One challenge I expect is that AI may not always understand emotions correctly, especially for complex songs. Also, managing and cleaning large data will be harder than working with 75 songs.

Overall, this step will help me understand how scaling changes both the process and meaning of data.
